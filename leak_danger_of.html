When you detect water leaks early, you will save your money and prevent potential disaster. Below are some of the signs of water leaks: 

HIGHER WATER BILLS 

Suppose there is no apparent reason for your water bill increase, you need to start keeping track of your water usage when no one is home. Note down the meter reading before you leave home and confirm whether there is any water leak.

REDUCED WATER PRESSURE

 Consider checking for water leaks in case the water pressure reduces. Some of the symptoms of reduced water pressure can include faulty valves or water leaks.

MOLD AND MILDEW

 Toxic black mold may develop due to water leaks. The mold is caused by the condensation of water or moisture from the water leaks. Black Mold also emits a musty odor that causes wood and drywall damage or even respiratory diseases.

PEELING PAINT OR WALLPAPER

 When your paint or add wallpaper peels, it may be an indication that there is a water leak. Your wall may be damaged if the water leaks are left unattended.

DAMAGED FLOORING

If your bathroom floor has a soft spot or the kitchen tiles are loose, there may be a water leak. Also, if areas with a carpet become moist and have a musty feel, you may have water leaks.

SOUND OF RUNNING WATER

 It is a good idea to check the toilet valves and faucets if you hear the sound of running water. In case they are okay, you may want to confirm your meter reading to verify whether there is water usage. If the water reader changes after some time and nobody is using the water, then it indicates a water leak.

OVERGROWTH IN YOUR LAWN

If your lawn has wet spots or some areas of your grass have become overgrown, you need to confirm whether or not you have a water leak. Leaks can cause serious lawn destruction.

VISIBLE WATER DAMAGE

In case you see damaged pipes, you need to fix them to avoid water leaks.
